2. Overview

Telescope Align ("the app") guides users step by step through aligning a telescope — including location, polar alignment, horizon obstacles, and star recommendations. The app works entirely offline and has no server backend. This privacy policy explains what data is processed while you use it.

3. No Server, No Account, No Cloud

The app has no backend and no user accounts. No data is transmitted to us or to any third party. All calculations (star positions, visibility, alignment steps) run exclusively on your device.

4. Data Processed

4.1 Location Data

When you use GPS positioning, the app requests your location through the standard browser interface (Geolocation API). This position is processed only locally on the device to compute star altitude and visibility. It is not stored, not logged, and never transmitted to a server. Alternatively, you can enter your location manually — in that case no GPS request is made.

4.2 Locally Stored Data

Your obstacle profile (which compass directions are blocked by trees, buildings, etc.) is stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage on your device. This data never leaves your device and is accessible only to you. You can remove it at any time via your browser settings or by clearing the site data.

4.3 Technical Data

When the app is loaded via GitHub Pages, only the usual anonymized access logs kept by GitHub are generated server-side (e.g. IP address, timestamp). We have no control over this; the GitHub Privacy Statement applies.

5. Offline Functionality (Service Worker)

The app uses a service worker to cache content for offline use (Progressive Web App). Only static app files (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, the star catalog) are stored in the browser cache — no personal data.

6. No Sharing With Third Parties

Since no data is collected or transmitted, none is shared with third parties. The app contains no advertising, no tracking, and no analytics software.

7. Your Rights

Because no personal data is stored outside your device, most data-protection rights of access or erasure are effectively moot — there is nothing on our side to delete that we never held in the first place. Any locally stored data (the obstacle profile) can be deleted by you at any time via your browser or app settings.
